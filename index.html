What is a secured credit card?

A secured credit card requires a cash deposit that acts as your collateral. Your deposit typically becomes your credit limit.

  • • Deposit $100–$500+ depending on issuer
  • • Spend like a normal credit card
  • • Builds your credit score with responsible use
  • • You get your deposit back when upgraded or closed

Who benefits from a secured card?

People with low/no credit

Ideal if you have no credit history or a damaged score.

Those rebuilding credit

Consistent on-time payments rebuild your score over time.

Students & newcomers

A secured card can be the first step into the financial system.

People needing limit control

Your deposit means you choose your own starting limit.

How issuers manage secured cards

  • • They hold your deposit in case of missed payments
  • • They report usage to credit bureaus monthly
  • • They may auto-upgrade you to an unsecured card after 6–18 months
  • • They monitor spend, repayment and utilization rate

When can you upgrade?

Most issuers review your account for an upgrade after:

  • • 6–12 months of on-time payments
  • • Low utilization
  • • No late fees
